About Me

I am an instructional designer and learning technology professional who creates practical training for adult learners, technical users, and workforce teams.

I focus on translating complex topics into clear learning paths, job aids, practice activities, and performance support. My experience includes AI literacy training, technical tool training, SharePoint/user adoption, onboarding support, documentation, and facilitation.

My design style is direct, learner-centered, and job-focused. I care about what learners need to do after training - not just what they need to know during training.

How I Design Learning

1

Clarify the problem

Identify the performance gap, audience needs, constraints, stakeholders, and desired business outcome.

2

Design for practice

Write measurable objectives and build activities that let learners apply skills in realistic situations.

3

Support transfer

Create job aids, checklists, resource hubs, and follow-up tools that help learners perform after training.

4

Evaluate and improve

Use feedback, knowledge checks, performance tasks, and stakeholder input to refine learning experiences.
